Mean (Average)

Add all values together and divide by how many there are. Use mean when your data is roughly symmetric — no extreme outliers.

🦷 Dental Example Ages of 5 patients: 25, 30, 34, 40, 41 → Mean = (25+30+34+40+41) ÷ 5 = 34 years

Median

Sort all values, then pick the middle one. Use median when data is skewed (e.g. pocket depths, DMFT scores) — it isn't pulled by outliers.

🦷 Dental Example Pocket depths: 2, 3, 4, 6, 9 mm → Median = 4 mm (middle value after sorting)

Measures of Spread (Variability)

These tell you how consistent or spread out your data is.

Standard Deviation (SD)

Shows how far values typically are from the mean. Small SD = patients are similar. Large SD = patients vary a lot. Reported as Mean ± SD in dental papers.

🦷 Dental Example Plaque index = 1.6 ± 0.4 → Most patients score between 1.2 and 2.0

Interquartile Range (IQR)

IQR = Q3 − Q1. It covers the middle 50% of your data and ignores extreme values — perfect for skewed clinical measurements.

🦷 Dental Example Median pocket depth = 4 mm, IQR 3–6 mm → 50% of patients have depths between 3 and 6 mm

Range

Range = Maximum − Minimum. Quick to calculate but sensitive to outliers.

How to Report Results in a Paper
📐

Normal data
Report as Mean ± SD
e.g. Age: 34.5 ± 8.2 yrs

📦

Skewed data
Report as Median (IQR)
e.g. Pocket depth: 4 (3–6) mm

📋

Categories
Report as Frequency (%)
e.g. Smokers: 42 (34%)
